Hovhannes Partizpanyan

Violin

Biography

Hovhannes Partizpanyan was born in 1986 in Yerevan, Armenia. At the age of six, he began playing the violin at the Sayat-Nova Children's Music School in Yerevan (initially in the class of V. Seyranyan, later with H. Kerobyan).
From 2001 to 2003, he studied under Prof. P. Haykazyan at the P. Tchaikovsky Specialized Music School.From 2003 to 2008, he continued his studies with Prof. E. Tadevosyan at the Yerevan State Conservatory.
From 2011 to 2017, he was a student in the class of Prof. M. Egelhof at the Lübeck University of Music.
While studying at the music school, Hovhannes Partizpanyan participated in the Sargis Aslamazyan–Avet Gabrielyan Competition in 2001, where he was awarded third prize.
In the same year, he became a member of the charitable foundation "New Names." Between 2000 and 2011, he performed numerous solo and chamber music concerts throughout Armenia and was a regular participant in the "Naregatsi Art Center" music festival, directed by A. Talalyan.
From 2003 to 2007, Hovhannes Partizpanyan was a member of the violin ensemble named after G. Adjemyan.
Between 2005 and 2008, he played in the "Alan Hovhaness" Chamber Orchestra, initially as principal second violin and later as concertmaster.
Subsequently, he held a position in the first violin section of the Armenian Philharmonic Orchestra from 2010 to 2011.
He is currently a permanent member of the Schleswig-Holstein State Theater and Symphony Orchestra.
Hovhannes Partizpanyan was a recipient of the DAAD Matching Funds Scholarship and a scholarship from the Possehl Foundation in Lübeck. He has participated in masterclasses with Nikolay Madoyan, Pavel Vernikov, Almuth Schlicker, and Saschko Gawriloff.
